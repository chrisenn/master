Payments and Invoicing

Research Case Study

Research Case Study

The business's core product was a two-sided marketplace to connect consumers who need a job done with local service providers (e.g. carpenters, photographers, removalists, etc.) who are available to do the work.

Business Problem

As a result of the organisation pivoting to a different revenue model, there was a business need to collect transaction data (which often occurred offline).

Research Objective

Establish the existing mental model regarding payments for jobs. Ascertain why certain payment methods are preferred and if there are any opportunities to improve the experience in an online setting.

Methodology

  • Research: Understand users and context.
    • Surveys
    • Interviews
  • Analysis: Draw insights from research.
    • Affinity mapping
    • Personas
  • Synthesis: Explore solutions
    • Scenarios
    • Design studios/ideation workshops
  • Delivery: Implementation
    • MVP
    • Usability Testing
    • Iteration

Key Insights

  • Customers generally wanted to do what was easiest for the business. Businesses had the higher status in the transaction.
  • Businesses were very anxious about getting paid, and they hated doing admin and chasing people up.
  • Cash was preferable for smaller jobs and/or where the cost was known. It was the quickest option if you had enough cash on hand.
  • Bank transfer was preferable for large and/or less predictable amounts. It was traceable and didn't require withdrawing money beforehand, but it carried the risk of delayed payment.
  • While customers would have liked to pay with card, accepting credit cards wasn't worth it for businesses

Solution

Our solution was allowing businesses to take credit card payments at no extra cost to allow customers more flexibility. We also aimed to streamline the invoicing/payment process to a quick in-person interaction at the end of the job. That is, using a combination of a verbal prompt from Mick and a push notification/SMS to request and authorise an electronic payment. This combines the immediacy and social accountability of cash with the flexibility and traceability of a bank transfer.
